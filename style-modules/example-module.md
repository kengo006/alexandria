## Punctuation
- Serial (Oxford) comma: yes.
- Em-dashes: at most one pair per paragraph; prefer colons or parentheses for asides.
- Quotation marks: double for quotes, single for scare quotes; punctuation inside per US convention.

## Banned constructions
- "It is important to note that…", "It should be mentioned that…" — state the thing.
- "Arguably" as a hedge without an argument attached.
- Rhetorical questions as paragraph openers.

## Quota rules
- "not X but Y" contrast frames: **at most 2 per piece** — the pattern implies exclusivity that arguments rarely earn. Approved variants: "X gives way to Y", "less X than Y", "X captures part of it; Y supplies the rest", two parallel declarative sentences.
- Sentence-initial "However": at most 1 per ~500 words; rotate with "Yet", "Still", clause-internal "though".

## Terminology conventions
- Technical terms of art: italicise on first occurrence, plain thereafter.
- Foreign-language terms: italics + gloss in parentheses on first occurrence.
- Settled per-project translations/renderings: see the project's terminology table (the table wins over memory).

## Idiom policy
- Idioms and set phrases: default omit in academic register; permitted only when they *name a concept* more precisely than plain words would. When unsure, plain words.
